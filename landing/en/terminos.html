2. What ZodHub Pulse is

The app is a maintenance and cleaning utility for personal computers running macOS and Windows. Its purpose is to give you visibility into what is taking up space on your machine and how it is performing, and to let you remove whatever you decide is surplus.

It runs entirely locally: scans execute on your device and neither your files nor the results are transmitted to any server. It requires no registration, no user account and no permanent internet connection to operate.

The software is provided free of charge. There is no paid tier, no features behind a wall, no advertising and no device limit.

3. What each module does

So you know exactly what you're allowing, here is what the app does, section by section. Features marked as exclusive to one system do not appear on the others.

  • Home: shows real-time CPU, memory, disk and network usage, temperatures from available sensors and the heaviest processes. Read-only; it changes nothing.
  • Free up space: finds accumulated junk — caches, temporary files, leftovers from development tools, Docker images and containers, the bin — and lets you delete whatever you select.
  • Storage: breaks down what fills the disk by volume and area, with a usage history. Read-only.
  • Explorer: walks folders computing their real size to find the heaviest items, and lets you move things to the bin or reveal them in the system file manager.
  • Duplicates: compares files by their content (cryptographic fingerprint), not by name, and groups the repeats so you can remove whichever copies you choose.
  • Applications: lists installed programs with their size, finds the files they leave behind and lets you uninstall them. On Windows it invokes the program's own official uninstaller.
  • Memory: details RAM and swap usage and offers a purge of inactive memory. "Memory pressure" is an approximation and is labelled as such in the interface.
  • Snapshots (macOS only): lists local Time Machine copies and lets you thin them using the system's own tools.
  • .DS_Store (macOS only): compresses folders without those hidden files and sweeps them away.
  • Tasks: schedules periodic maintenance using the operating system's own scheduler (launchd on macOS, Task Scheduler on Windows).
  • Your space: project news, voluntary newsletter sign-up, a support link and the legal texts.
